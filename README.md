Hindgalian — Language Family and Ancient State
8 languages. Over 2000 years of evolution. Three writing systems.

Hindgalian is not a single language but an entire language family, discovered by researcher Hindic. The languages were not invented — they were uncovered and described through reconstruction of the evolutionary tree.

Evolution of the Languages
In the beginning was Old Hindgalian. From it came Historic Hindgalian — an ancient form with cases and dual number. Then New Hindgalian, a modern simplification.

From New Hindgalian, four branches split off.

First — Kintarino, a melodic language where all words end in vowels, featuring an evidentiality marker indicating that the speaker did not witness the event firsthand.

Second — Huntacica, a transitional language with an octal numeral system. Its verbs end in -kh or -th. The language is now extinct.

Third — Motar, the first language in the family to use the Cyrillic script. It features vowel harmony.

Fourth — Belgiushi, a tonal language with five tones: high, low, falling, rising, and flat. All verbs begin with the letter p.

Motar later evolved into Hindorusska — a living language with Russian grammar. It is spoken by 68 percent of Hindgalian speakers.

The Ancient State of Hindgalia
More than 2000 years ago, there existed a state called Hindgalia. It occupied twelve islands and the mainland of Kintar. The state was anarchic — there was no president, no parliament, no police, no army, no taxes. The prince was elected and had a single vote, equal to everyone else's. All eight languages were recognized as equal.

The economy was based on common ownership of land, water, and forests. Exchange was voluntary. The currency was called the Kintar, symbolized by Ⱪ.

Hindgalia disappeared over 2000 years ago. Today, only linguistic records remain.

Discovery
The Hindgalian language family and the records of the ancient state were discovered — not invented — by researcher Hindic. Through analysis of historical documents and grammatical reconstruction, he uncovered the evolutionary tree, the writing systems, and the grammar of all eight languages.
